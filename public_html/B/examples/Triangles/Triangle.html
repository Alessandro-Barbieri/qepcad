Q E P C A D - A Triangles Problem

More information will eventually appear here! What I want to say about things is this: The problem below is essentially a simple theorem about triangles. The algebraic conditions on the side lengths a,b,c come from applying the law of cosines on a simple condition on two of the angles of this triangle, namely that the angle of the vertex opposite side c is greater than half the external angle of the vertex opposite side b. The input below gives that algebraic condition along with the assumption that a,b,c really are side lengths of some non-degenerate triangle. We simplify the result, and we get a nice clean characterization in sides of what was originally specified in angles, namely that c > a.

This problem is a nice example of why declaring assumptions in QEPCAD B can be a very good thing. Since the assumptions are, naturally enough, assumed by the solution formula rather than appearing in the solution formula, the formula that returns is very easy to interpret.
